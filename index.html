Should a mortal designate another as their proxy, that proxy shall be granted one credit—a mark of borrowed power, etched into the ledger. Yet the one who surrenders their proxy shall inherit a debt of will, mortally bound to the soul until balance is restored. Their name becomes shackled to the proxy’s authority. A debt incurred through proxy designation can only be lifted by assigning an equivalent magnitude of proxy to another. Until such an action is taken, the original debtor remains bound to the obligation.
